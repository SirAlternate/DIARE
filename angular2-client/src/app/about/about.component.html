About DIARE

Overview

DIARE stands for "Digital Interpretation of Analog Rapid-logging Entries". We know, it's a mouthful. The site is based off the Bullet Journal framework, which was created by Ryder Carroll. Bullet Journal prides itself on being an analog system for rapidly logging data entries, and this website is the digital interpretation of that system. DIARE, and the framework that it is based on, aim to simplify and speed up the traditional note-taking and journaling process. We hope that our application enables you to live a more efficient lifestyle!

Getting Started

Have you never used the Bullet Journal framework before, or just have never used DIARE? That's okay, we have your back! Follow the steps below to get up and running.

Creating A New Account

If you haven't created an account with DIARE yet, head on over to the Registration page to sign up. You only need two things in order to sign up for an account: a valid email address and a password. Click the submit button once you've filled the registration form out. If your account was successfully created, you'll be taken to this About page. If something went wrong, you'll see an error message letting you know what happened.

Logging In

To login to DIARE, go here. Fill out your account email address and password, and then click the submit button. If your login was successful, you'll be taken to your Journals page. Otherwise, you'll be shown an error message explaining what went wrong.

Modifying or Deleting Your Account

Once you've logged into your account, click on the Account tab in the site navigation bar. A dropdown menu will appear. Click the Settings option in the dropdown, which will take you to the account Settings page. There are two forms on the settings page: the first allows you to change your email address and the second allows you to change your password.

In order to delete your account, scroll down to the bottom of the settings page and click the red Delete Your Account button. A confirm dialog will pop up. If you click the Cancel button in this dialog, no changes will be made. If you click the "Yes, Delete My Account" option, all of your journal data and account data will be completely wiped from our database. We do not keep any of your data when you remove your account.

Creating, Modifiying, and Deleting Journals

Journals can be created, modified, and deleted from the Journals page, which is the first page you see when you log into the site. The My Journals sidebar can be used to create a new journal. Type in a title for your new journal in the Journal Title textfield, and then either hit your Enter key or press the Create Journal button to create the journal.

In order to edit the title of your journal, first locate the journal that you'd like to modify in the My Journals sidebar. Next, click on the button that looks like three vertical dots, which will bring up a menu of journal actions that can be performed. Click on the Edit Title option, which will display a popup form with a text field for entering the new title. Fill this field out, and then click the Submit button to make the change. If you change your mind about editing the journal title, you can hit the Cancel button or the "x" in the top right corner of the dialog to cancel any changes. If you go through with changing your journal title, you will see the journal title automatically change in the My Journals sidebar upon submission.

If you'd like to delete your entire journal and all of its contents first open the menu for that journal, as discussed in the paragraph above. Click on the Delete option of the menu, which will ask you if you really want to delete the journal. If you click the Cancel button, no changes will be made. If you click "Yes, Delete The Journal," your journal will be completely removed from the database, and you will see the journal disappear from the sidebar.

Creating, Modifying, and Deleting Pages

To create a page, click on the menu for the journal you want to add it to (the button with the three vertical dots), and then click on the Add Page option. A pop up dialog form will appear, allowing you to enter a page title. Upon clicking the submit button, the page will automatically be created and displayed in your My Journals sidebar.

You have two options when it comes to editing a page title. You can either click on a page title edit directly in the page or you can edit it via the page's menu in the sidebar. To edit the page title directly from the page, simply click on title text, change it, and then hit enter. To change it from the page's sidebar menu, click the Edit Title option, fill out the form that pops up, and click the Submit button. The page title will immediately update for either workflow you choose.

To delete a page, click on the page's menu and then select the Delete Page option. A confirmation dialog will appear, and you can either approve the deletion or cancel it. If you approve the deletion, the page will automatically be deleted and it will disappear from your display.

Creating, Modifying, and Deleting Bullets

To create a new bullet, simply use the toolbar at the top of the page that you're on. You can decide wether to favorite the bullet or not, what type of bullet it will be, and what the text of the bullet will say. When you're done filling out the bullet form, you can either hit the Enter key or click the Add Bullet button. You will immediately see the bullet on the page.

To edit a bullet, click on the bullet's text. You can then change the bullet content and hit enter to save your changes. Right now you cannot edit the bullet type or whether the bullet is a favorite or not. This will be added in future iterations.

To delete a bullet, hover over the grey DELETE button to the right of the bullet's text. The DELETE button will turn red. If you then click on the button, the bullet will be removed, without any prior confirmation dialog. We chose not to provide a confirmation dialog for deleting a bullet in order to keep bullet actions a quick as possible.

Performing a Search

Currently, you can perform a search across all of your bullets, from all journals. The search will take your query parameter and find all of your bullets that contain that parameter. You can access the search feature from the Search tab in the site's navigation bar. The search page will be expanded in the future so that more advanced searches can be performed.
